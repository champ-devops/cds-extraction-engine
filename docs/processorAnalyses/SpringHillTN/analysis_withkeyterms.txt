  Most names (Murray, Davis, Carter, Napier, Allen, etc.) were already transcribed correctly and show no delta.

  Other observations

  - The key term version has 238 fewer words and 142 fewer cues — suggesting the model also changed segmentation/chunking behavior, not just spelling
  - The biggest beneficiaries are Fuqua (corrected from "Fuquay" consistently) and Canepari (corrected from two different misspellings)
  - Early in the transcript there are also minor text quality differences unrelated to names (e.g., "5:45pm" → "5:45 p.m.", "Alderman" vs "Aldermen")
