  ---
  Formatting & Readability (Critical for Captions)

  Rev.ai — Almost zero punctuation. Sentences run together. Filler words (uh, um) are kept throughout, and there is a non-standard <affirmative> tag that is not valid VTT. Poor readability for viewers.

  Deepgram — Good punctuation where text exists, but cues are fragmented into 1–2 second chunks, often just one or two words. This produces choppy, unreadable captions that flash too quickly on screen. Unsuitable for
   closed captioning without post-processing.

  AssemblyAI — Best sentence structure and punctuation. Cue sizes are appropriate (~5s). Filler words are mostly cleaned up. Resolution numbers are formatted numerically. Most consistent and readable output.

  ---
  Verdict

  AssemblyAI is the best option for closed captioning accuracy and readability. It has the cleanest formatting, fewest word errors, best proper noun recognition, and caption cue sizes that are appropriate for
  on-screen display.

  Rev.ai is second in word-level accuracy but is nearly unusable as-is due to missing punctuation and filler words — it would need significant post-processing.

  Deepgram produces the most fragmented output (2,190 cues vs ~1,500 for the others) and has the most word-level errors among the three. It is the weakest option for this use case without significant reformatting.

  ---
  One caveat: The Alderman's name (spelled variously as Canari / Canapari / Canipari / Kanapari) is likely a locally-known name that none of the services confidently recognized. All three got it wrong or
  inconsistently — this is a known limitation of AI transcription for local proper nouns.
